# LAWYER RESEARCH DIRECTORY
## Finding the Right Legal Counsel for AuditsReady

**Goal:** Find 2-3 qualified SaaS/technology lawyers to interview for legal document review and ongoing counsel.

---

## IDEAL LAWYER PROFILE

### Must-Have Qualifications
- ✅ **SaaS/Technology law experience** (50%+ of practice)
- ✅ **GDPR compliance expertise** (critical for worldwide service)
- ✅ **5+ years practicing** (preferably with startup/SaaS focus)
- ✅ **Responsive communication** (email/phone within 24-48 hours)
- ✅ **Reasonable rates** ($200-600/hour depending on location)

### Nice-to-Have Qualifications
- ✅ AI/ML product liability knowledge
- ✅ ISO/compliance industry familiarity
- ✅ Professional engineering (P.Eng) liability experience
- ✅ Multi-jurisdictional experience (Canada, US, EU)
- ✅ Data Processing Agreement (DPA) experience

### Red Flags to Avoid
- ❌ Primarily litigation (not transactional/advisory)
- ❌ No SaaS or tech experience
- ❌ No GDPR experience
- ❌ Unresponsive or slow communication
- ❌ Rates >$700/hour (unless top-tier and worth it)
- ❌ Requires $10k+ upfront retainer for small engagement

### 4. Online Legal Platforms (Budget-Friendly Options)

**Upwork**
- Website: https://www.upwork.com
- Search: "SaaS lawyer," "GDPR lawyer," "technology attorney"
- Pros: Wide range, competitive pricing ($50-200/hour)
- Cons: Quality varies, no ongoing relationship
- Best for: Document review on a budget
- **How to use:**
  1. Post a job: "SaaS Legal Document Review (Terms, Privacy, Agreements)"
  2. Require: 5+ years experience, SaaS portfolio, GDPR knowledge
  3. Budget: $3,000-$6,000 fixed price
  4. Review proposals, interview top 3

**Priori Legal**
- Website: https://www.priorilegal.com
- Description: Vetted lawyer marketplace for startups
- Pros: Pre-screened lawyers, startup-focused, transparent pricing
- Cons: May not have Alberta-specific lawyers
- Rates: $200-500/hour
- Best for: US-based or multi-jurisdictional needs

**Atrium**
- Website: https://www.atrium.co
- Description: Tech-forward legal services for startups
- Pros: SaaS-native, subscription model, tech-friendly
- Cons: US-focused
- Pricing: $500-2,000/month subscriptions
- Best for: Ongoing legal support

**LegalZoom / Rocket Lawyer** (DIY + Lawyer Review)
- Website: https://www.legalzoom.com or https://www.rocketlawyer.com
- Pros: Low cost ($200-500), templates included
- Cons: Generic templates, limited customization
- Best for: Very early stage, bootstrap budget
- **Recommendation:** Use only for business formation, NOT for custom SaaS terms

## RECOMMENDED SEARCH STRATEGY

### Week 1: Research & Outreach

**Day 1-2: Research (3-5 hours)**
1. Google search: "Calgary SaaS lawyer," "Alberta technology lawyer," "GDPR lawyer Canada"
2. Check Alberta Law Society directory for tech lawyers
3. Ask for referrals in local startup communities (LinkedIn, Slack groups)
4. Browse Upwork for budget-friendly options
5. Identify 5-7 potential lawyers

**Day 3-4: Outreach (2 hours)**
1. Send introductory emails to 5-7 lawyers (use template in EMAIL-TO-LAWYER.md)
2. Request 30-min consultation call
3. Provide LAWYER-REVIEW-SUMMARY.md for context

**Day 5-7: Screen Responses**
1. Review responses for responsiveness and rates
2. Narrow to top 3 candidates
3. Schedule 30-45 min video calls

### Week 2: Interviews & Selection

**Day 8-12: Interviews**
1. Conduct 3 interviews using LAWYER-INTERVIEW-QUESTIONNAIRE.md
2. Take detailed notes
3. Compare using scorecard

**Day 13-14: Decision**
1. Check 1-2 references for top candidate
2. Negotiate pricing if needed
3. Request engagement letter
4. Sign and pay retainer

## EVALUATION CRITERIA

When comparing lawyers, prioritize:

**1. Relevant Experience (40% weight)**
- SaaS/technology law experience
- GDPR compliance expertise
- AI/ML liability knowledge

**2. Communication & Fit (30% weight)**
- Responsive and clear communication
- Understands startup/bootstrap mentality
- Practical advice (not overly cautious)

**3. Pricing (20% weight)**
- Transparent and reasonable rates
- Fixed fee or clear estimate
- Value for money

**4. Availability (10% weight)**
- Can start within 1-2 weeks
- Reasonable turnaround (2-3 weeks for review)

## QUESTIONS TO ASK YOURSELF

Before hiring:

1. **Do they "get" the business?**
   - Do they understand SaaS business models?
   - Do they appreciate the AI/compliance context?

2. **Do I trust their judgment?**
   - Are they practical or overly cautious?
   - Do they explain risks clearly?

3. **Can I afford them?**
   - Is the pricing transparent and reasonable?
   - Are there hidden costs?

4. **Will they be responsive?**
   - Did they respond promptly during outreach?
   - Do they commit to reasonable turnarounds?

5. **Do I want to work with them long-term?**
   - Can I see them as ongoing counsel?
   - Do they offer retainer/subscription models?
