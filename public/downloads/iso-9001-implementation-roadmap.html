Month 1: Foundation & Planning

Foundation PhaseClauses 4, 5, 6 | Establish QMS framework

Week 1-2: Clause 4 - Context of Organization

  • Identify internal/external issues: Conduct SWOT analysis covering equipment, labor, cash flow, regulations, customer requirements, competitors
  • Document interested parties: List stakeholders (customers, employees, regulators, suppliers, investors) and their needs
  • Define QMS scope: Write scope statement covering products/services, locations, and exclusions
  • Create process flow diagram: Map core processes from receiving through shipping with department interactions

Week 3: Clause 5 - Leadership

  • Draft quality policy: Write 3-5 sentence statement reflecting your business and commitment to quality
  • Get management sign-off: CEO/Owner approves and commits to quality policy
  • Communicate policy: Post in break room, add to employee handbook, discuss in team meeting
  • Document roles: Create organizational chart with quality responsibilities for each position

Week 4: Clause 6 - Planning (Risk & Objectives)

  • Create risk register: Identify 5-10 quality risks (equipment failure, supplier issues, key employee turnover)
  • Document risk mitigation: For each risk, define preventive actions and controls
  • Set quality objectives: Define 3-5 measurable goals (defect reduction, on-time delivery, customer satisfaction)
  • Create change management process: Document how you'll handle major operational changes

üì¶ Month 1 Deliverables:

  • Context document (internal/external issues, interested parties)
  • QMS scope statement
  • Process flow diagram
  • Quality policy (approved and posted)
  • Roles & responsibilities document
  • Risk register with mitigation plans
  • Quality objectives (measurable)
2

Month 2: Documentation & Infrastructure

Implementation PhaseClause 7 | Build QMS support systems

Week 1-2: Document Control System (Clause 7.5)

  • Choose documentation platform: Select electronic system (Google Drive, SharePoint, dedicated QMS software)
  • Create document control procedure: Define version control, approval process, distribution, retention
  • Set up folder structure: Organize by clause or process (Procedures, Work Instructions, Forms, Records)
  • Inventory existing documentation: List all current procedures, work instructions, forms, and records

Week 2-3: Resources & Competence (Clause 7.1-7.2)

  • Confirm resource adequacy: Verify sufficient staff, equipment, and budget for QMS
  • Document infrastructure: List buildings, equipment, IT systems critical to operations
  • Create calibration log: Identify measuring equipment requiring calibration, schedule calibrations
  • Document training needs: For each position, list required competencies and training
  • Create training records template: Design form to track employee training (what, when, who, signature)

Week 4: Gap Analysis

  • Compare existing docs to requirements: Identify what procedures are missing or incomplete
  • Create documentation priority list: Rank which procedures to write/update first (focus on Clause 8)
  • Assign writing tasks: Delegate procedure writing to subject matter experts

üì¶ Month 2 Deliverables:

  • Document control procedure
  • Electronic documentation system set up
  • Existing documentation inventory
  • Competency matrix by position
  • Training records template
  • Calibration schedule and records
  • Gap analysis report with priority list
3

Month 3: Operations Documentation

Implementation PhaseClause 8 | Document core processes

Week 1: Customer-Related Processes (Clause 8.2)

  • Document customer communication: How customers contact you, how you respond, what records you keep
  • Create order review procedure: How you confirm you can meet specs, delivery, and price before accepting orders
  • Design order review form: Template for capturing customer requirements and internal approval

Week 2: Design Control (Clause 8.3) - If Applicable

  • Document design process: Inputs, reviews, validation, verification, change control
  • Create design forms: Templates for design reviews, validation, and changes
  • OR exclude design: If you don't design products, document exclusion in scope

Week 3: Supplier Control (Clause 8.4)

  • Create approved supplier list: Document evaluation criteria and current approved suppliers
  • Document purchasing process: How you create POs, communicate specs, verify receipts
  • Create receiving inspection procedure: How you inspect incoming materials and handle rejections

Week 4: Production & Service Provision (Clause 8.5)

  • Document production planning: How you schedule work, allocate resources, control output
  • Create/update work instructions: For critical operations, document step-by-step procedures
  • Implement product identification: Job numbers, lot codes, serial numbers throughout production
  • Document final inspection: Who approves, what's checked, pass/fail criteria
  • Create nonconformance procedure: How you handle defects (scrap, rework, customer approval)

üì¶ Month 3 Deliverables:

  • Customer communication procedure
  • Order review procedure and form
  • Design control procedure (or exclusion documented)
  • Approved supplier list and evaluation criteria
  • Purchasing procedure
  • Receiving inspection procedure
  • Production planning procedure
  • Work instructions for critical processes
  • Product identification system
  • Final inspection procedure
  • Nonconformance procedure
4

Month 4: Training & System Rollout

Implementation PhaseTrain team & start generating records

Week 1-2: Employee Training

  • Schedule training sessions: Plan training for all employees on new/updated procedures
  • Conduct management training: Train leadership on management review, objectives, commitment
  • Train document control: Ensure employees know how to access latest procedures
  • Train production staff: Walk through work instructions, inspection procedures, nonconformance reporting
  • Document all training: Sign training records for every session (who, what, when, signature)

Week 2-3: Implement Performance Monitoring (Clause 9.1)

  • Set up metrics tracking: Create spreadsheet or system to track quality objectives
  • Define data collection methods: Who collects what data, how often, where it's stored
  • Start tracking metrics: Begin recording defect rates, on-time delivery, customer complaints, etc.
  • Implement customer satisfaction measurement: Launch surveys, scorecard system, or complaint tracking

Week 3-4: Generate Records

  • Start using new procedures: Ensure employees follow documented processes
  • Collect records: Build evidence of compliance (order reviews, inspection records, calibrations, training)
  • Organize records systematically: File in electronic system per document control procedure
  • Address non-compliance: If employees aren't following procedures, retrain and correct

üì¶ Month 4 Deliverables:

  • Training records for all employees (signed)
  • Quality metrics tracking system operational
  • Customer satisfaction measurement launched
  • 30+ days of quality records (inspections, calibrations, orders, etc.)
  • Evidence of procedures being followed
5

Month 5: Internal Audit & Management Review

Validation PhaseClauses 9.2-9.3 | Test QMS effectiveness

Week 1: Prepare for Internal Audit

  • Appoint internal auditor: Select trained person (can be employee or hire external auditor)
  • Create audit schedule: Plan to audit all QMS clauses and key processes
  • Develop audit checklist: List questions to verify compliance with each clause
  • Notify departments: Inform employees when their area will be audited

Week 2: Conduct Internal Audit (Clause 9.2)

  • Opening meeting: Kick off audit with management and department heads
  • Audit all processes: Interview employees, review records, observe operations
  • Document findings: Note nonconformances (major/minor) and opportunities for improvement
  • Closing meeting: Present findings to management

Week 3: Corrective Actions (Clause 10.2)

  • Assign corrective actions: For each finding, assign owner and due date
  • Investigate root causes: Use 5 Whys or fishbone diagram to find why problems occurred
  • Implement corrections: Fix immediate issues and address root causes
  • Document corrective actions: Track actions, evidence of implementation, verification of effectiveness

Week 4: Management Review (Clause 9.3)

  • Prepare management review package: Compile metrics, audit results, customer feedback, resource needs
  • Conduct management review meeting: CEO/leadership reviews QMS performance and effectiveness
  • Document decisions: Record management decisions, action items, resource commitments
  • Communicate outcomes: Share management review results with organization

üì¶ Month 5 Deliverables:

  • Internal audit report with findings
  • Corrective action plans (assigned and in progress)
  • Root cause analysis for each finding
  • Management review minutes
  • Management review action items

‚ö†Ô∏è Critical: Do NOT schedule certification audit until all internal audit corrective actions are closed. Certification auditors will verify that your internal audit was conducted properly and that you addressed findings.

6

Month 6: Certification Audit Preparation

Validation PhaseFinal readiness & certification

Week 1-2: Close Corrective Actions

  • Verify all CARs closed: Ensure corrective actions from internal audit are implemented
  • Verify effectiveness: Check that corrections actually fixed the problems
  • Update audit report: Document closure of findings

Week 2: Select Certification Body

  • Get quotes from 3 CBs: Request proposals from accredited certification bodies
  • Verify accreditation: Ensure CB is accredited by recognized body (ANAB, UKAS, etc.)
  • Select certification body: Choose based on price, reputation, auditor expertise
  • Schedule Stage 1 audit: Book date for document review (usually 1 day on-site)

Week 3: Pre-Audit Readiness Assessment

  • Conduct mock audit: Simulate certification audit with consultant or experienced auditor
  • Check document availability: Ensure procedures are accessible to auditor
  • Review records completeness: Verify you have 3+ months of records for all processes
  • Prepare employees: Brief team on what to expect during audit
  • Fix any last-minute gaps: Address issues found in mock audit

Week 4: Certification Audit (Stage 1 & 2)

  • Stage 1 audit: Auditor reviews documentation and readiness (1 day)
  • Address Stage 1 findings: Fix any issues identified before Stage 2
  • Schedule Stage 2 audit: Book full certification audit (2-4 days depending on size)
  • Stage 2 audit: Full QMS audit including interviews, record reviews, process observations
  • Closing meeting: Auditor presents findings and recommendation
  • Address minor NCs: If minor nonconformances found, submit corrective actions within 30 days
  • Receive certificate: Once approved, certification body issues ISO 9001 certificate

üì¶ Month 6 Deliverables:

  • All internal audit corrective actions closed
  • Certification body contract signed
  • Stage 1 audit completed
  • Stage 2 audit completed
  • ISO 9001:2015 certificate issued üéâ

After Certification: Surveillance & Maintenance

Congratulations on achieving ISO 9001 certification! But remember: certification is not the finish line. It's the starting point for continual improvement.

Ongoing Requirements:

Activity Frequency Purpose
Surveillance Audits Annual (Years 1 & 2) CB verifies you're maintaining QMS
Recertification Audit Every 3 years Full audit to renew certificate
Internal Audits Minimum annually Required by Clause 9.2
Management Review Minimum annually Required by Clause 9.3
Metric Tracking Ongoing (monthly recommended) Monitor quality objectives
Corrective Actions As needed Address nonconformances

üí° Best Practice: Schedule recurring calendar reminders:

  • Internal audit planning (90 days before anniversary)
  • Management review (30 days before anniversary)
  • Surveillance audit scheduling (60 days before anniversary)
  • Calibration due dates
  • Training refreshers

Common Reasons for Certificate Suspension:

  • Skipping internal audits: You must conduct them annually, no exceptions
  • Ignoring corrective actions: Repeated findings show QMS not improving
  • Not following procedures: If auditor finds procedures aren't being followed
  • Customer complaints spike: Major quality issues can trigger special audits
  • Ignoring surveillance audits: Missing or rescheduling without reason

Continual Improvement Opportunities:

  • Automate record-keeping with QMS software
  • Integrate ISO 9001 with other standards (ISO 14001, IATF 16949, AS9100)
  • Use AI tools to analyze quality data trends
  • Engage employees in improvement initiatives (Kaizen, Six Sigma)
  • Benchmark against industry best practices
